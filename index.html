1936

Berlin: the Olympics as a Nazi ideological showcase

Berlin hosted the 1936 Summer Olympics under Adolf Hitler’s regime, and the event was carefully staged to impress foreign visitors with images of order, grandeur, and national strength. Nazi authorities treated the Games as an international display window for the regime and its racial ideology, even if that message was partly challenged by the victories of athletes such as Jesse Owens.

This case shows that a sporting mega-event can function as a tool of state propaganda. It also reveals a lasting tension at the heart of the Olympics: universalist rhetoric does not prevent political instrumentalization.
